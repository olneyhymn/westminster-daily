#### Confession of Faith

##### Chapter 2: Of God, and of the Holy Trinity

There is but one only,[^fnref:wcf1] living, and true God,[^fnref:wcf2] who is infinite in being and perfection,[^fnref:wcf3] a most pure spirit,[^fnref:wcf4] invisible,[^fnref:wcf5] without body, parts,[^fnref:wcf6] or passions;[^fnref:wcf7] immutable,[^fnref:wcf8] immense,[^fnref:wcf9] eternal,[^fnref:wcf10] incomprehensible,[^fnref:wcf11] almighty,[^fnref:wcf12] most wise,[^fnref:wcf13] most holy,[^fnref:wcf14] most free,[^fnref:wcf15] most absolute;[^fnref:wcf16] working all things according to the counsel of his own immutable and most righteous will,[^fnref:wcf17] for his own glory;[^fnref:wcf18] most loving,[^fnref:wcf19] gracious, merciful, long-suffering, abundant in goodness and truth, forgiving iniquity, transgression, and sin;[^fnref:wcf20] the rewarder of them that diligently seek him;[^fnref:wcf21] and withal, most just, and terrible in his judgments,[^fnref:wcf22] hating all sin,[^fnref:wcf23] and who will by no means clear the guilty.[^fnref:wcf24]
