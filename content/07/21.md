#### Shorter Catechism

55\. What is forbidden in the third commandment?

A. The third commandment forbiddeth all profaning or abusing of anything whereby God maketh himself known.[^fnref:wsc1]


[^fnref:wsc1]: <div class="esv"><h5>Leviticus 19:12</h5> <div class="esv-text"><p id="p03019012.01-1">You shall not swear by my name falsely, and so profane the name of your God: I am the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.</p> </div><h5>Matthew 5:33-37</h5> <div class="esv-text"> <p id="p40005033.02-2"><span class="woc">&#8220;Again you have heard that it was said to those of old, &#8216;You shall not swear falsely, but shall perform to the Lord what you have sworn.&#8217;</span> <span class="woc">But I say to you, Do not take an oath at all, either by heaven, for it is the throne of God,</span> <span class="woc">or by the earth, for it is his footstool, or by Jerusalem, for it is the city of the great King.</span> <span class="woc">And do not take an oath by your head, for you cannot make one hair white or black.</span> <span class="woc">Let what you say be simply &#8216;Yes&#8217; or &#8216;No&#8217;; anything more than this comes from evil.</span></p> </div><h5>James 5:12</h5> <div class="esv-text"><p id="p59005012.01-3">But above all, my brothers, do not swear, either by heaven or by earth or by any other oath, but let your &#8220;yes&#8221; be yes and your &#8220;no&#8221; be no, so that you may not fall under condemnation. #### Larger Catechism

113\. What are the sins forbidden in the third commandment?

A. The sins forbidden in the third commandment are, the not using of God's name as is required;[^fnref:wlc1] and the abuse of it in an ignorant,[^fnref:wlc2] vain,[^fnref:wlc3] irreverent, profane,[^fnref:wlc4] superstitious,[^fnref:wlc5] or wicked mentioning or otherwise using his titles, attributes,[^fnref:wlc6] ordinances,[^fnref:wlc7] or works,[^fnref:wlc8] by blasphemy,[^fnref:wlc9] perjury;[^fnref:wlc10] all sinful cursings,[^fnref:wlc11] oaths,[^fnref:wlc12] vows,[^fnref:wlc13] and lots;[^fnref:wlc14] violating of our oaths and vows, if lawful,[^fnref:wlc15] and fulfilling them, if of things unlawful;[^fnref:wlc16] murmuring and quarreling at,[^fnref:wlc17] curious prying into,[^fnref:wlc18] and misapplying of God's decrees[^fnref:wlc19] and providences;[^fnref:wlc20] misinterpreting,[^fnref:wlc21] misapplying,[^fnref:wlc22] or anyway perverting the Word, or any part of it,[^fnref:wlc23] to profane jests,[^fnref:wlc24] curious or unprofitable questions, vain janglings, or the maintaining of false doctrines;[^fnref:wlc25] abusing it, the creatures, or anything contained under the name of God, to charms,[^fnref:wlc26] or sinful lusts and practices;[^fnref:wlc27] the maligning,[^fnref:wlc28] scorning,[^fnref:wlc29] reviling,[^fnref:wlc30] or anywise opposing of God's truth, grace, and ways;[^fnref:wlc31] making profession of religion in hypocrisy, or for sinister ends;[^fnref:wlc32] being ashamed of it,[^fnref:wlc33] or a shame to it, by unconformable,[^fnref:wlc34] unwise,[^fnref:wlc35] unfruitful,[^fnref:wlc36] and offensive walking,[^fnref:wlc37] or backsliding from it.[^fnref:wlc38]
