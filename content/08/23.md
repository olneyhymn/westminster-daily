#### Shorter Catechism

<span class="q">78.</span> What is forbidden in the ninth commandment?

<span class="q">A.</span> The ninth commandment forbiddeth whatsoever is prejudicial to truth, or injurious to our own, or our neighbor's, good name.[^fnref:wsc1]

#### Larger Catechism

<span class="q">145.</span> What are the sins forbidden in the ninth commandment?

<span class="q">A.</span> The sins forbidden in the ninth commandment are, all prejudicing the truth, and the good name of our neighbors, as well as our own,[^fnref:wlc1] especially in public judicature;[^fnref:wlc2] giving false evidence,[^fnref:wlc3] suborning false witnesses,[^fnref:wlc4] wittingly appearing and pleading for an evil cause, outfacing and overbearing the truth;[^fnref:wlc5] passing unjust sentence,[^fnref:wlc6] calling evil good, and good evil; rewarding the wicked according to the work of the righteous, and the righteous according to the work of the wicked;[^fnref:wlc7] forgery,[^fnref:wlc8] concealing the truth, undue silence in a just cause,[^fnref:wlc9] and holding our peace when iniquity calleth for either a reproof from ourselves,[^fnref:wlc10] or complaint to others;[^fnref:wlc11] speaking the truth unseasonably,[^fnref:wlc12] or maliciously to a wrong end,[^fnref:wlc13] or perverting it to a wrong meaning,[^fnref:wlc14] or in doubtful and equivocal expressions, to the prejudice of truth or justice;[^fnref:wlc15] speaking untruth,[^fnref:wlc16] lying,[^fnref:wlc17] slandering,[^fnref:wlc18] backbiting,[^fnref:wlc19] detracting,[^fnref:wlc20] talebearing,[^fnref:wlc21] whispering,[^fnref:wlc22] scoffing,[^fnref:wlc23] reviling,[^fnref:wlc24] rash,[^fnref:wlc25] harsh,[^fnref:wlc26] and partial censuring;[^fnref:wlc27] misconstructing intentions, words, and actions;[^fnref:wlc28] flattering,[^fnref:wlc29] vainglorious boasting;[^fnref:wlc30] thinking or speaking too highly or too meanly of ourselves or others;[^fnref:wlc31] denying the gifts and graces of God;[^fnref:wlc32] aggravating smaller faults;[^fnref:wlc33] hiding, excusing, or extenuating of sins, when called to a free confession;[^fnref:wlc34] unnecessary discovering of infirmities;[^fnref:wlc35] raising false rumors,[^fnref:wlc36] receiving and countenancing evil reports,[^fnref:wlc37] and stopping our ears against just defense;[^fnref:wlc38] evil suspicion;[^fnref:wlc39] envying or grieving at the deserved credit of any,[^fnref:wlc40] endeavoring or desiring to impair it,[^fnref:wlc41] rejoicing in their disgrace and infamy;[^fnref:wlc42] scornful contempt,[^fnref:wlc43] fond admiration;[^fnref:wlc44] breach of lawful promises;[^fnref:wlc45] neglecting such things as are of good report,[^fnref:wlc46] and practicing, or not avoiding ourselves, or not hindering what we can in others, such things as procure an ill name.[^fnref:wlc47]
