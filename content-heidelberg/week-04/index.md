## Lord's Day 4

### God's Justice and Mercy

*Human Misery*

#### Question 9

Does not God then do injustice to man, by requiring from him in his law, that which he cannot perform?

##### Answer

Not at all; [^fn9a] for God made man capable of performing it; but man, by the instigation of the devil, [^fn9b] and his own wilful disobedience, [^fn9c] deprived himself and all his posterity of those divine gifts.

#### Question 10

Will God suffer such disobedience and rebellion to go unpunished?

##### Answer

By no means; but is terribly displeased [^fn10a] with our original as well as actual sins; and will punish them in his just judgment temporally and eternally, [^fn10b] as he has declared, "Cursed is every one that continueth not in all things, which are written in the book of the law, to do them." [^fn10c]

#### Question 11

Is not God then also merciful?

##### Answer

God is indeed merciful, [^fn11a] but also just; [^fn11b] therefore his justice requires, that sin which is committed against the most high majesty of God, be also punished with extreme, that is, with everlasting punishment of body and soul.
