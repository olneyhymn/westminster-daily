## Lord's Day 40

### The Sixth Commandment

*Christian Gratitude*

#### Question 105

What does God require in the sixth commandment?

##### Answer

That neither in thoughts, nor words, nor gestures, much less in deeds, I dishonour, hate, wound, or kill my neighbour, by myself or by another: [^fna] but that I lay aside all desire of revenge: [^fnb] also, that I hurt not myself, nor wilfully expose myself to any danger. [^fnc] Wherefore also the magistrate is armed with the sword, to prevent murder. [^fnd]

#### Question 106

But this commandment seems only to speak of murder?

##### Answer

In forbidding murder, God teaches us, that he abhors the causes thereof, such as envy, [^fna] hatred, [^fnb] anger, [^fnc] and desire of revenge; and that he accounts all these as murder. [^fnd]

#### Question 107

But is it enough that we do not kill any man in the manner mentioned above?

##### Answer

No: for when God forbids envy, hatred, and anger, he commands us to love our neighbour as ourselves; [^fna] to show patience, peace, meekness, mercy, and all kindness, towards him, [^fnb] and prevent his hurt as much as in us lies; [^fnc] and that we do good, even to our enemies. [^fnd]
